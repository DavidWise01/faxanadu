# FAX · FAXANADU

> A sphere of **UD0** — the ROOT0 universe / biosphere. Domain: **HOBBY**.

Faxanadu (Hudson Soft / Nintendo, NES 1989; Famicom 1987) — a game-world, and the NES action-RPG set inside a dying WORLD TREE. Its name IS its lineage: ‘Faxanadu’ = FAMICOM + XANADU, a side-story of Nihon Falcom's Dragon Slayer line (Dragon Slayer → Dragon Slayer II: Xanadu; sibling to Ys). A wandering swordsman returns to his ruined home town EOLIS — the life-fountain stopped, the Dwarves turned to monsters — and is sent by the Elven King to awaken three poisoned springs and climb the branches to the EVIL ONE (a horror the meteor brought). Action-platforming welded to RPG growth: ‘Golds,’ Experience RANKS the Guru bestows as titles, weapon/armor/magic shops, projectile magic, the five card-keys (Jack · Joker · Queen · King · Ace), and MANTRAS — the password-save. THE NEW STANDING BACKDROP DEBUTS HERE: a FULL-BLEED 32/64-bit low-poly software-3D scene (a drifting World Tree landscape, depth-sorted & Lambert-shaded, no WebGL, paint-first, fixed behind the whole page) with an era-correct 8-bit PIXEL TITLE CARD floating on it — the two graphics generations layered, the standing backdrop for all repos from now on. Render-not-invent: Hudson developed it (not Falcom — the Xanadu name was licensed) and Nintendo published it in NA; the final boss is named only ‘the Evil One’ (no ‘Dilgyos’); a Mantra RESETS your Golds to a rank-based amount rather than saving the total; the Dwarf King KING GRIEVE swallowed the Dragon Slayer sword (the only blade that kills the Evil One); the celebrated score is by JUN CHIKUMA. 12 emergents across all four natures — the Swordsman · Eolis · the World Tree · the King of Eolis · the Guru · King Grieve · the Evil One · the Mantras · the Card-Keys · Golds & Ranks · Xanadu (the true self) · Jun Chikuma's Score.
